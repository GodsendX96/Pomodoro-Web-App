About Pomodoro

What's Pomodoro?

Pomodoro is a time management technique that relies on using a timer to break up work into 25 minute segments accompanied by 5 minute breaks; after 4 "Pomodoros" you take a longer break, usually between 15 to 30 minutes long. The technique was developed and popularized by the famous author Francesco Cirillo in the late 80s. The word "Pomodoro" comes from the italian term for tomato! Cirillo came up with the name back when he used a tomato-shaped timer during his college days!

How-To Pomodoro

It's easy, I promise!

To Pomodoro you simply...

  1. Choose a task you want to pomodoro
  2. Start first pomodoro (25 mins)
  3. Take a short break (5 mins)
  4. After 4 pomodoros do a long break (15 mins)
  5. Repeat!!! (You'll get used to it!)

Why Pomodoro

How useful is pomodoro really?

In a modern society such as ours, it is important to keep track of our work whether we're trying to get our homework done or finally do that project our bosses wanted us to start a week ago! Pomodoro helps us complete our tasks in a timely matter without stressing ourselves out. It also teachs us to take breaks often and helps us stay focused when we really need to be! Plus it feels a lot more rewarding knowing that you broke down such large projects into bite-size chunks! Well, at least that's how I feel about it anyway..
